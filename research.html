Binary Black Holes

Since June of 2021 I have been working on a self led project performing high accuracy 3-body graviational simulations of Binary Black Holes in AGN. Specifically, I simulated the effects of gaseous dynamical friction (via Ostriker Friction) providing a new mechanism of efficient captures from a wide range of impact parameters. This work was completed under the combined gracious guidance of Prof. Zoltan Haiman and PhD student Marguerite Epstien-Martin.

Stellar Activity with Hα

Since February of 2020 I have been working with Prof. Marcel Agüeros on Hydrogen Alpha (Hα) Emissions. Through my work with him and the Columbia University Cool Stars group I was able to present an iPoster at the 237th AAS meeting, contributed to the development of PHEW 2.0 (a package to effectively and semi-automatically measure Hydrogen-alpha equivalent widths in spectra) and was second author on Sabine Chu's RNAAS.



A sample of a set of spectra that I reduced, plotted, measured with PHEW 2.0. I then analyzed these kinds of stellar spectra from LAMOST and other different surveys to create a new catalog for my work on Hα time variaibility.
